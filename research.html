Impact of Pharmaceutical Manufacturing on India's Drinking Water

The pharmaceutical industry is globally connected and impacts every aspect of modern life. The industry and its influence can be broken up into 4 distinct sectors, which includes production, distribution, consumption, and waste. This paper explores and models this system of distribution production.

Applications of Hydrogels formed from Spider Silk Proteins

Hydrogel polymers that are synthesized from spider silk proteins are environmentally friendly and possess a wide range of mechanical advantages. These mechanical advantages include high levels of yield stress, high elastic and shear moduli, high tensile strengths, and other properties.

The Sustainability and Environmental Impact of Popular Motorsports

Both NASCAR and Formula 1 have made advancements towards increasing their sustainability efforts and performance. This paper explores the thermodynamic modeling of both types of engines and characterizes the carbon emissions from each motorsport.

Effectiveness of Metformin: Room Temperature vs. Refrigerated

Metformin is commonly prescribed for people who have Type 2 diabetes where the body cannot control its blood sugar levels naturally. The drug works by activating the body’s response to insulin, the chemical that naturally controls glucose levels. This paper explores the phase equilibrium between refrigerated metformin vs room temperature using a thermodynamic approach of fugacity and Gibb's Free energy.
