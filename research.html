Distribution can be expanded to include the scale up of chemical formulas and the physical supplying of pharmaceuticals to hospitals and drugstores. Project 2

Applications of Hydrogels formed from Spider Silk Proteins--Guided by: Dr. Connor Bilchak

Background Info

Hydrogel polymers that are synthesized from spider silk proteins are environmentally friendly and possess a wide range of mechanical advantages. These mechanical advantages include high levels of yield stress, high elastic and shear moduli, high tensile strengths, and other properties. Likewise, spider silk found in nature possesses its individual mechanical advantages, such as high tensile strengths, which contributes to the mechanical performance of the hydrogels. These properties are associated with the cross-linked structure that forms from the synthesis of the silk proteins. The mechanical properties can be optimized for biomedical applications such as creating surgical meshes that can withstand high levels of stress from human bone or an absorptive capsule that can capture large volumes of fluid. The Sustainability and Environmental Impact of Popular Motorsports--Guided by: Dr. James Patrick Abulencia

Question Posed: Which popular motorsport, NASCAR or Formula 1, leaves a larger carbon footprint on local communities?

Background Info

Both NASCAR and Formula 1 have made advancements towards increasing their sustainability efforts while ensuring top performance for drivers during races. For instance, NASCAR invested in multiple renewable energy sources to power their facilities across the nation. They have constructed a solar farm house with, “40,000 solar panels and even provides energy to the local grid to power 250 homes, thereby reducing total carbon dioxide emissions in the area by 2,370 tons annually,” (ERA Environmental). Formula 1 has also made advancements and initiated its Net-Zero plan to offset its emissions and reduce its carbon footprint. “Formula 1 says the fuel will be made using components from either carbon capture, municipal waste, or non-food biomass,” (SciBlog.org, 2022). Essentially, these “waste” products include distillate plastics, which are then synthesized to be usable as a fuel source. Project 4

Effectiveness of Metformin: Room Temperature vs. Refrigerated--Guided by: Dr. James Patrick Abulencia

Question Posed: Would the effectiveness of a metformin oral solution increase or decrease if it were to be stored at a refrigerated temperature as opposed to room temperature?

Background Info

Metformin is commonly prescribed for people who have Type 2 diabetes where the body cannot control its blood sugar levels naturally; insulin injection not necessarily needed. The drug works by activating the body’s response to insulin, the chemical that naturally controls glucose levels. By activating the body’s insulin response, the substance essentially decreases the amount of glucose you absorb from food and the glucose produced in the liver. The main components in both the metformin capsule and oral solutions are hydrogen, carbon, chlorine, and nitrogen. Diabetes is a disease that can induce life threatening health problems if one doesn’t take the proper medication or treatment. The interaction between metformin and the body to control glucose levels is essential to maintaining a healthy blood sugar level in instances where the body cannot regulate itself.
