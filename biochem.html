Biochemistry Methods: Recombinant DNA & DNA Purification

Protein Background

Native Proteins

  • Facilitate many biological processes (gene expression, cell growth & nutrient uptake)
  • Can undergo post-translational modifications
  • Expression: DNA→RNA→Amino Acid codon→ protein

Recombinant Proteins

  • Recombinant/modified amino acid sequences to express a specific protein
  • Requires host/medium for protein growth ( bacteria, yeast, mammalian cells)
  • Recombinant expression: vector → modified → cloned → transferred to host → protein

Note a Similarity

Any protein can be expressed in a cell that has the same DNA codon sequence for the amino acids

Recombinant Protein Expression

Purpose of Recombinant Proteins

  • Manipulate native form of protein to increase protein production, modify genetic sequences
  • Increases intracellular protein abundance and concentration
  • Cleave the plasmid at a specific location and splice in a codon that codes for a specific protein

Host

Rate of growth (fast or slow) & easy manipulation of cellular functions

Expression Vector

Modified version of extrachromosomal DNA (plasmids) that can do autonomous replication in bacterial cells

Selection Marker

Protein that offers resistance any toxins present in the growth medium where protein is being expressed
