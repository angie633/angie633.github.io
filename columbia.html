Research Area: Ultrasonic Transducer Technology to Increase Drying Rate Efficiency

NIR Spectroscopy Experiments

I conducted NIR spectroscopy experiments on both an absorbent polymer sphere and a small porous sponge. The setup included a light-sensitive chamber with a light probe attached at the top. A 3D apparatus was needed to hold the polymer while it was drying.

Purpose of Experiment

The goal was to monitor the change in wavelength of the material as it was air drying to determine if the rate would increase or decrease based on the amount of water in the sample.

Transducer Drying Setup

This setup included a chamber where the polymer sphere was dried while resting on top of an ultrasonic transducer. The transducer emitted vibrational sound waves over the same length of time as the NIR setup.

Purpose of Experiment

The goal here was to determine if the drying rate of the polymer would increase when ultrasonic sound waves were present over a specific length of time.

Driver Circuit & Levitation

This setup was the driver circuit that allowed for the power output to the transducers. By varying the frequency of the sound waves, the object between the transducers (paper) was able to levitate between the nodes and anti-nodal positions.

Purpose of Experiment

The goal was to determine the optimal frequency of vibration for the sound waves to dry the objects.

3D Printing Projects

COMSOL Simulations

This is a collection of the COMSOL simulations that were made to model the drying process. Each one examines a different aspect of drying using ultrasonic waves versus without ultrasonic waves (in ambient conditions).
