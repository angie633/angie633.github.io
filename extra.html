Summer 2023
Columbia University: REU Experience (Moment & Kymissis Lab)

Research Area: Ultrasonic Transducer Technology to Increase Drying Rate Efficiency

Designed and facilitated experiments for ultrasonic transducer sound waves to increase drying efficiency while reducing energy consumption

NIR 3D Printed Box

A 3D printed box that would hold the NIR sample and probe. Created using AutoCAD and Prusa slicing software.

3D Printed Orbeez Holder

The blue rectangle plate was 3D printed to hold the sample inside the NIR setup. Design specs were determined using AutoCAD.

Orbeez Drying Apparatus

The drying apparatus consisted of a scale, a small 3D printed box to hold the orbeez sample, and a driver circuit connected under the box. The goal was to monitor the weight of the sample as it dried while being supplied a specific frequency. At the optimal frequency, the sample would dry faster while using less energy.

NIR Spectroscopy Setup

The NIR Spectroscopy setup included a light probe and a chamber to hosue the sample. The probe detects light wavelengths of specific colors depending on the sample type. For the polymer "Orbeez" I studied, the wavelengths would decrease over time as the amount of light reflecting off the sample decreased as it dried.

Driver Circuit

This circuit was designed with a "driver" sink. This setup was used to determine the optimal frequency and voltage for a faster drying rate. It was also used to determine the power and efficiency of the specific transducer being used.

COMSOL Multiphysics Modeling

This was a model rendering to test the thermodynamics and heat transfer properties of drying rates with an ultrasonic transducer. The blue areas represent exposure to outside air whereas the green/yellow areas represent the effects of the sound waves to dry the material. Explored vapor-liquid equilibrium modeling in terms of drying efficiency.

Summer 2024
Columbia University: Protein Engineering (Banta Lab)

Research Area: A.ferrooxidan Cell Classification for Bioleaching/Biomining

Performed experiments to classify ferrooxidans cells and their impact on bio-leaching processes for extraction of rare-earth elements from mining pollution

Gel Results for Ligation

3-step PCR process to create a vector with tetracycline resistance.
PCR 1: Cuts out ampicillin from pSB536.
PCR 2: Primes tetracycline strand.
PCR 3: Ligates tetracycline into the parent vector.

Filters for sm4 Media

Filtration was conducted for various cell growth media to balance osmotic conditions and study ferrooxidan activity.

Hemocytometry: Cell Counting in 9K Media

Cells were counted to measure ferrooxidan growth in 9K basal salt media, using Lugol iodine stain for visibility.

Media Formulation and pH Testing

Created media for ferrooxidan growth, including 9K basal salt media and sulfur-containing sm4 media.

PCR Ligation Setup

Optimized plasmid ligation setup for testing resistance in E.coli cells, including redesign of primer sequences.

DNA Mini-Prep/Extraction

Performed DNA extraction from overnight cultures and agarose samples, followed by spectrophotometer analysis.
